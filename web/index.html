The Data-driven Innovation Platform.

The next-generation platform for data-driven innovation

Innovationsfonden Grand Solutions 2018

Why

Data-driven Innovation (DI) is essential to be competitive for both businesses and the public sector. However, two obstacles prevent its large-scale adoption:

  • DI requires integrating different data sources (existing databases, sensors) with different methods for data analytics and visualisation. Integration is expensive (>50% of total costs) and cannot be reused.
  • DI is resource-intensive and event-based: heavy computations are triggered automatically by the arrival of new data. These computations run on clouds. But optimising cloud costs is extremely difficult and most companies end up wasting money: 35+% of cloud resources are paid for, but not needed.

What

In this project, we will develop the Data-driven Innovation Platform (DIP), a next-generation cloud technology for programming and deploying data-driven software. The DIP will dramatically lower integration and running costs of data-driven cloud software, by integrating and bringing to the market two new technologies developed at the University of Southern Denmark (Jolie and Zephyrus2).

Using the DIP, we will develop and implement the following.

  • A cloud execution engine for running software on the cloud with optimised resource usage, commercialised by Sentia.
  • A dashboard product for data-driven application, commercialised by Cerno.
  • A fault prediction tool for refrigerators, integrated at Danfoss (and their supermarket customers).
  • Traffic and event planning tools based on data science, to optimise traffic and events at Kolding and Nyborg municipalities.
  • A new cloud infrastructure for optimised data-driven projects at the SDU eScience Centre (Danish National High-Performance Computing Centre).
