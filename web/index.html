The Digital Innovation Office

Who are we

The Digital Innovation Office (DIO) fosters and manages collaborations centered around Digital Technology between the University of Southern Denmark (SDU), companies, and other organisations, both nationally and internationally. DIO provides the necessary technical, scientific, and pedagogical know-how to carry out these activities. We are an inclusive unit covering different degrees and topics related to digital technology, and we operate in close collaboration with the Research and Innovation Office at SDU for legal support and organisation.

Activities

The activities of DIO include the following.

  • Promoting collaborations in research and innovation, by supporting joint projects, meet-ups, and the creation of consortia.
  • Maintaining the SDU Digital Business Club, a registry of companies and other external organisations interested in collaborations on digital technology.
  • Managing the SDU Talent Fellowship and fostering further recruitment opportunities for both international and national students.
  • Discussing with businesses the potential for new (post-)education initiatives (e.g., intensive workshops), regarding digitalisation and computational science.
Examples of concrete initiatives include:
  • Exchange agreements between SDU and external organisations regarding knowledge, students, and resources.
  • Study activities and thesis projects at businesses for students.
  • Facilitating lecturer exchanges between SDU and Industry.
  • Career days and other matchmaking events for businesses and students.
  • Digital summer schools.

Competencies

The main technical areas of competence and expertise covered by DIO include Computer Science and Engineering, Big Data, Digitalisation, and Industry 4.0.

DIO’s competitive advantage:

  • Founders and maintainers of the International Microservices Community.
  • The eScience Centre: hosting and managing the national HPC Centre.
  • Exclusive partnership with Technology Denmark.

The main technical areas of competence and expertise covered by DIO include:

  • Algorithms and Efficient Computation.
  • Artificial Intelligence, including Machine Learning, Expert Systems, and Statistics.
  • Big Data and Data Science, including Bioinformatics, Cheminformatics, and Parallel Computing.
  • Digitalisation and System Integration.
  • Modern paradigms for software and IT infrastructures in Industry 4.0: Cloud Computing, Edge computing, DevOps, Internet of Things, Microservices, Dark Data.
  • Software development tools.
