Who are we

The Digital Innovation Office (DIO) fosters and manages collaborations on Digital Technology between the University of Southern Denmark (SDU), companies, and other organisations. Collaborations are both at national and international level. DIO provides the necessary technical, scientific, and pedagogical know-how to carry out these activities. DIO is an inclusive unit: it covers different degrees and topics related to Digital Technology and it operates in close collaboration with the Research and Innovation Office at SDU, which provides legal and administrative support.

Activities

The activities of DIO include the following.

  • Promoting collaborations in research and innovation, by supporting joint projects, meet-ups, and the creation of consortia.
  • Maintaining the SDU Digital Business Club, a registry of companies and other external organisations interested in collaborations on Digital Technology.
  • Managing the SDU Talent Fellowship and fostering further recruitment opportunities for both international and national students.
  • Discussing with businesses the potential for new (post-)education initiatives (e.g., intensive workshops), regarding digitalisation and computational science.
Examples of concrete initiatives include:
  • Exchange agreements between SDU and external organisations regarding knowledge, students, and resources.
  • Study activities and thesis projects at businesses for students.
  • Facilitating lecturer exchanges between SDU and Industry.
  • Career days and other matchmaking events for businesses and students.
  • Digital summer schools.

Competence & Competitive Advantage

The main technical areas of competence covered by DIO include Computer Science and Engineering, Big Data, Digitalisation, and Industry 4.0.

DIO combines world-level experts in:

  • Algorithms and Efficient Computation.
  • Artificial Intelligence, including Machine Learning, Expert Systems, and Statistics.
  • Big Data and Data Science, including Bioinformatics, Cheminformatics, and Parallel Computing.
  • Digitalisation and System Integration.
  • Modern paradigms for software and IT infrastructures in Industry 4.0: Cloud Computing, Edge computing, DevOps, Internet of Things, Microservices, Dark Data.
  • Software development tools.

Thanks to its collaborations and partnerships, DIO holds an outstanding position to help promoting innovation both at the technical and business levels:

  • It is part of the Microservices Community, an international group of experts (practitioners and researchers) in the software paradigm of Microservices: the state-of-the-art in building modern, large-scale systems.
  • It work in close collaboration with the eScience Centre, the host and manager of the Danish national HPC Centre.
  • It is an exclusive partner of Technology Denmark, a trade association devoted to attract, develop and retain Digital Technology talent.
